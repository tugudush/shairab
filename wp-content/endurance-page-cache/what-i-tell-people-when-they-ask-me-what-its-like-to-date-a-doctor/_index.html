What I Tell People When They Ask Me What Its Like to Date a Doctor

Posted on August 17, 2016August 15, 2017


First of all, this is not something that’s easy to write because I can already picture out some eyebrows raising. (Lol!) I  believe that only people with negativity wrapped around their heads would do that.

I was compelled to write this after encountering the question “What WAS it like to date a doctor?” again on a recent interview. The truth is I used to get a bit annoyed when this question or anything related to this comes up in random conversations but later on, I just laugh it out and shake it off!

It is amusing on how high the expectations are of people when it comes to dating a doctor. Some are really clueless as to how the medical world works so I would understand if the expectations are far from reality.

So  what is it really like to date a doctor? 

Since I am not foreign to the medical world (refer to the author’s bio!), I do know a thing or two about health care professionals. Dating one has its own perks and pitfalls.

1.By now, you know that most doctors work round the clock under any circumstances. Imagine going on a movie date that you have planned for weeks then your partner’s phone rings endlessly. Sounds great right? But you should not be annoyed (you just can’t!) because you signed up for this – dating someone who tries to save lives. That’s it! I can list a bunch of other scenarios but that would be like a litany. Most doctors are incredibly hardworking and are dedicated to what they do so you have to completely understand this to date one.

2.With white gown comes great responsibility and power. This is a gentle reminder to prepare on possibly dealing with a guy who gets a super ego boost  when he wears his white gown. Real talk – it makes someone feel more powerful but it also means a huge amount of responsibility on his shoulders. To be fair, they have the right to feel powerful because saving lives is an extraordinary accomplishment. We can only hope that humility  always prevails even with such power.

3.With great power comes great privilege. It’s a given that temptations will be lurking in the shadows. (You know what I mean!) I can only say one thing – “a doctor is more prone to infection” (literally and figuratively!) How he responds to whatever temptation is in front of him is not a reflection of his professional capacity but rather a reflection of his personal character.

4.Doctors are not paid very well in our country. This is a sad reality. Many people get the wrong impression that doctors even the young ones are rich or have high salary. Some of them come from well off families which is why they can afford to drive fancy cars or live luxurious lives even with a not so high pay (Don’t get me wrong – Many of them also live simple lives!) It takes many years for a doctor to build his career and earn well. Don’t expect too much! I usually hear people say “Oh wow, a doctor! He must be rich! You don’t need to work!” LOL! Seriously?! You have no idea what you are talking about. After  MANY years of work though, the income part changes dramatically for sure!

5. One of the perks of having a partner who is a doctor is having someone who knows exactly what to do and what medication to give you when you are not in your best physical condition. This gives anyone a feeling of safety and security.

6. Most doctors possess incredible interpersonal skills (case to case basis). Since a doctor deals with people every day, establishing rapport comes in an instant. This comes in handy when you introduce him to your family and friends. He easily becomes like-able. However, I have also met a lot of doctors who don’t possess IPR skills at all! ( I can give you a list!)

7. Dating is already scary. Dating a doctor can be scarier but if you think about it, everything in life is. It’s scary to trust your friends. It’s scary to move to new places. It’s scary to fall in love and get hurt but as they say, the scarier you get, the more exciting it should be.

Honestly, I have vowed to myself that I will not date a doctor anymore. I have had instance/s wherein a guy wouldn’t tell me that he is a doctor because he knows that I’ll probably turn him down. It sounds a bit absurd but we all have a certain type that fits us. I guess this one isn’t for me but YOU (yes, you – the one reading this) have a different chance and fate. To date a doctor is an implausible experience and this is something which will greatly help you understand relationships in a whole new level. Whatever happens in the end, let it be an experience that makes you better not bitter.
